Dhamma Corner

In a world characterized by the "NAVI" conditions—Non-linear, Accelerated, Volatile, and Interconnected—leaders often find themselves grasping for control amidst shifting geopolitical tides and technological surges. We see SoftBank investing billions, nations securing defense pacts, and prediction markets attempting to quantify the future. Yet, the Buddha's teaching on anicca (impermanence) offers a vital counter-perspective for the modern strategist.

The desire to build "resilient" supply chains or "sovereign" digital infrastructure is fundamentally an attempt to create a permanent structure in a world that is, by nature, in constant flux. When we attach ourselves to the success of a contract, the stability of a region, or the dominance of a technology, we create the very conditions for our own suffering when those things inevitably change. The Buddha does not suggest we abandon our responsibilities; rather, he teaches us to engage with them without the grasping mind that demands they remain static.

The Kaccanagotta Sutta provides a framework for navigating this complexity. It teaches the middle way between the extremes of eternalism (the belief that things can be made permanent) and nihilism (the belief that nothing matters). For the digital nomad or the market mover, this means building the data center, negotiating the agreement, and driving the innovation—but doing so with the clear understanding that these are transient phenomena. We act with full intensity, but we do not carry the burden of the outcome.

When the geopolitics of scarcity drive nations to compete for resources, the mind trained in the Dhamma sees the futility of greed. True security is not found in the stockpiling of minerals or the fortification of borders, but in the internal stability that remains untouched by the rise and fall of external circumstances. By recognizing the interconnected nature of all things, we stop seeing "rivals" to be defeated and start seeing systems in which we participate. This is the ultimate strategic advantage: a mind that is clear, flexible, and unburdened by the illusions of control.

Kaccanagotta Sutta: On Right View (SN 12.15)
Personal Reflection

Today’s news is filled with the frantic energy of building, defending, and predicting. We are constantly told that we must be faster, more resilient, and more prepared for the next disruption. It is easy to feel that if you stop running, you will be left behind by the current of progress. But ask yourself: are you running toward a goal that provides genuine meaning, or are you simply running to escape the fear of being still? You are more than the sum of your strategic decisions. Your worth is not a variable to be adjusted by market shifts.

*If you could not use your achievements or your plans to define who you are, what would remain?*
