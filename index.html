Dhamma Corner

In a world defined by rapid technological expansion and shifting geopolitical alliances, it is easy to become caught in the current of "becoming"—the relentless drive to secure, to build, and to assert influence. We see the nations of Southeast Asia maneuvering to become hubs of the new digital age, and we see global powers negotiating the flow of energy. All of this activity is rooted in the human desire for stability and the fear of losing ground. Yet, the Dhamma teaches us that this very search for a secure foundation in a world of impermanent phenomena is the root of our agitation.

When we look at the efforts to build massive digital corridors or to secure energy supply chains, we are observing the manifestation of the "self" on a grand scale. We are trying to build a fortress that will stand against the winds of change. The Buddha, however, pointed to the nature of dependent origination. Nothing stands alone. The success of an AI ecosystem in Southeast Asia is dependent on global energy, stable trade, and the labor of millions. When we realize this interconnectedness, the urge to grasp and control softens into a strategy of wise engagement.

For the decision-maker, the investor, or the innovator, the challenge is to act with full vigor while maintaining an inner stillness that is not dependent on the outcome. This is the practice of "action without grasping." You build the infrastructure, you negotiate the contracts, and you lead the teams—but you do so with the understanding that these structures are, by nature, subject to change. When you stop demanding that the world remain static, you become much more effective at navigating the flux.

Consider the teaching in the *Sabbasava Sutta*, which instructs on how to manage the influx of unwholesome states. In the context of business and strategy, this means recognizing when the drive for growth is fueled by greed or when the fear of competition is fueled by aversion. By observing these mental states, you can act with clarity rather than reaction. This is not passive; it is the highest form of strategic intelligence. It is the ability to see the board clearly because your own mind is not clouded by the need for a specific result.

As you navigate your day, bring this awareness to your decisions. Do not let the noise of the global news cycle dictate your internal weather. Build, innovate, and lead, but do so with the lightness of one who knows that the true foundation is not found in the world of external success, but in the clarity of a mind that is at peace with the reality of change.
